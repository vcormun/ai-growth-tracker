AI & growth scenario tracker

Where does today's measured AI automation rate place us on the Jones & Tonetti (2026) growth-explosion spectrum?

The macroeconomics of AI can be reduced, in the Jones & Tonetti task-based model, to a single statistic: the automation rate x₀ — the share of total labor cost accounted for by tasks newly handed to capital each year. That one number determines the degree of dynamic increasing returns Φ, and Φ decides whether growth stays balanced or accelerates toward a singularity. Jones & Tonetti could not observe x₀, so they elicited it from frontier LLMs. The Anthropic Economic Index measures it from real usage. This tracker turns that measurement into the scenario picture, and is designed to refresh with each Economic Index release.

How it works

Jones & Tonetti show that the degree of dynamic increasing returns maps to observable data through one approximation:

Φ ≈ ( ψ̇ℓ + x₀ / (1 − σ) ) / gY0

When Φ < 1, growth is semi-endogenous and converges to a balanced path at rate g = Φn / (1 − Φ). At Φ = 1 the model is on the knife-edge of endogenous growth. When Φ > 1, income reaches infinity in finite time — though "weak links" (σ well below 1) keep even the explosive case slow.
